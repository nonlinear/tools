    How do others audit?

    Library and publisher auditors use the Portico audit interface for a variety of purposes.

    We have found that our publisher auditors will often come to see how much of their content has been processed, how the volume and issue listings and table of contents are being displayed, and what their articles look like when rendered for end-users.

    Library auditors have varied purposes. Some librarians will use the audit interface to audit Portico in the true sense of the word. They will, in advance, identify a set of specific articles to review and then compare them at Portico and at the publisher's site to confirm that all of the content has been preserved.

    Other librarians use the audit interface to check on the general status of Portico. They will set-up a reminder for themselves and come back every couple of months to confirm that content is still moving into the archive at a reasonable rate.

    Many librarians will also use the Portico audit interface as they plan for deaccessioning print volumes. In such cases, they are particularly interested in determining whether or not all of a journal has been preserved.

    Can I audit in a way that helps Portico?

    Yes! Portico is particularly interested in two areas of feedback:

    1. Is the rendition reasonable or do some areas look incorrect? And
    2. how complete is the journal and does it appear that content is missing?

    Portico welcomes comments on these two areas or any others.
