    Why don't the articles look like they do at the publisher?

    Portico preserves the original source files as provided by the publisher, not the HTML rendition. Portico performs two initial transformations on e-journal articles:

    1. we transform the source XML files into the NML archival standard and
    2. we transform the article packaging to an archival format.

    These two steps allow Portico to uniformly manage the archive and delivery of content.

    Why does the audit interface look different from a delivery site?

    The purpose of the audit interface is quite different from that of a delivery site. The audit interface allows librarians and publishers to check-in on Portico's status and the status of specific content, whereas a delivery interface is all about getting the right content to the right user as fast as possible. The Portico delivery interface is also available to Portico auditors. In addition, once an auditor is auditing a specific article, one of the options is to view the item as it would be rendered for delivery, which provides a preview of how the article would look when delivered to end users.

    What will my users see when content is triggered or made available via a perpetual access (or post-cancellation access claim)?

    The Portico delivery interface is available for you to review. All institutions should see, at least, the triggered titles. If you would like to see just one or two articles, once you are auditing a specific article or book, choose to view the article or book as it would be rendered and that will display the content as it would be provided in the delivery interface.
