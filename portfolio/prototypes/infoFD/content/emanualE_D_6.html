Maintaining a safe work environment requires the continuous cooperation of all employees. The Company strongly encourages employees to communicate with fellow employees and their supervisor regarding safety issues.

All employees are entitled to Workers' Compensation benefits pursuant to New York law. This coverage is automatic and immediate and protects you from an on-the-job injury. An on-the-job injury is defined as an accidental injury suffered in the course of your work, or an illness, which is directly related to performing your assigned job duties. FreshDirect pays for this job-injury insurance. If you cannot work due to a job-related injury or illness and you meet requirements by reporting promptly and accurately, Workers' Compensation insurance pays your medical bills and provides a portion of your income until you can return to work.

All injuries or illnesses arising out of the scope of your employment must be reported to your immediate supervisor promptly. Prompt reporting is the key to prompt benefits. Nothing can happen until your employer knows about the injury. Ensure your right to benefits by reporting every injury promptly, no matter how slight. Failure to report accidents is a serious matter as it may preclude an employee’s coverage under Worker’s Compensation Insurance.

Workers' Compensation payments for lost wages are subject to certain time limits and restrictions determined by applicable law. You should consult with the Human Resources Department for details.

Employees seeking to return to work after being absent due to a work-related injury must report to their immediate supervisor prior to beginning work and must provide a written doctor's clearance for returning to work to Human Resources before returning to work.
