FreshDirect operates under the principle of at-will employment. This means that neither you nor FreshDirect has entered into a contract regarding the duration of your employment. You are free to terminate your employment with FreshDirect at any time, with or without reason, and with or without notice. Likewise, FreshDirect has the right to terminate your employment at any time, with or without reason, and with or without notice.

FreshDirect hopes and expects that you will give at least two (2) weeks advance notice in the event of your resignation. The giving of such notice will qualify you to be considered an employee who left in good standing. Any accrued but unused PTO, up to a maximum of 10 days, will be paid to you at the time of employment termination, provided that you give such two week notice. Failure to provide such notice will result in you not receiving such unused PTO time.

Resignation/Termination

An employee who intends to resign should notify his or manager or supervisor in writing at least two weeks in advance. If the employee provides notice but the Company does not require the employee during all or part of the notice period, the employee is not entitled to pay for time not worked. After giving notice of resignation, employees are expected to assist the Company by providing information concerning their current projects and help in the training of a replacement. During the last two weeks, the employee may not use paid time off except paid holidays. If the employee does not provide advance notice as requested, the employee will not be eligible for rehire.

The Company will determine if the terminating employee has any outstanding debt owed to the Company and whether the individual has in his/her possession any Company credit cards, uniforms, tools, keys, safety equipment, manuals, vehicles, ID cards/badges or other Company property. Any employee terminating employment is expected to return any Company property in his/her possession.

A final paycheck for time worked (less applicable deductions and withholdings) will be issued to the employee on the next regularly scheduled pay date in accordance with the applicable federal and state law.
