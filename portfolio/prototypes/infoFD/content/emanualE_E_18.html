  • Maternity Leave and Paternity Leave

Maternity leave is a paid leave associated with the birth of an employee's own child or the adoption of a child. In the case of the birth of an employee's child, maternity leave applies to birth mothers only. In the case of adoption, maternity leave applies to the child's primary care giver only. Maternity leave is eight consecutive weeks, and is in addition to an employee's use of accumulated PTO. Maternity leave eligibility is limited to (a) permanent, salaried employees (b) who are otherwise eligible for Family Medical Leave Act ("FMLA") leave, as described below in this manual, and (c) who meet the other requirements set forth in this section.

The eight weeks of leave shall be paid weekly on the same cycle as normal payroll. The employee must provide 30 days notice (or as much notice as practicable if the leave is not foreseeable) to her department head and must complete and file the necessary forms with the Human Resource Department.

Maternity leave shall begin, at the employee's election, up to two weeks before an employee's scheduled due date or adoption date, but must begin no later than the actual date of birth or date of adoption. In any case, Human Resources must be notified as soon as possible to the exact start date. PTO if available or FMLA leave if applicable shall be used prior to the beginning of maternity leave when medically necessary.

After the eight weeks of maternity leave have concluded, subsequent leave shall be covered under other applicable leaves and/or policies. Maternity leave runs concurrently with leave under the FMLA or other applicable unpaid leave but runs consecutively with paid time off under PTO. Leave not covered by maternity leave or PTO is unpaid.

For leave under this section, the Company's reinstatement commitments set forth under the FMLA section, below, will extend to 16 weeks instead of 12 for other FMLA leaves.
