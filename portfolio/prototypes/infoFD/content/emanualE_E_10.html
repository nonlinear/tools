FreshDirect supports our country’s judicial system and our employees’ duty to participate. If an employee is summoned to appear and possibly be chosen for a jury, the employee must notify his/her manager and the Human Resources Department immediately and provide a copy of the summons. The employee will be granted leave to appear for jury duty. He/she will be required to show Human Resources the court’s work excuse document that will be given to the employee when he/she is excused from participation or at the end of the trial. Failure to provide this excuse document will result in the employee being charged PTO hours.

FreshDirect will pay the employee his/her regular rate of pay (less any allowance given by the court) for up to three (3) working days. If jury duty exceeds the three-day limit, the employee will either use PTO time or go on a leave of absence without pay. If you wish to be excused from jury duty, please inform the Human Resources Department and you will be issued an excuse letter that the court may or may not accept. FreshDirect also reserves the right to ask the court for permission to excuse you from jury service if FreshDirect needs your services on those days.

You will only be paid for jury duty on days you are scheduled to work. If you are scheduled to work on weekends, you are not excused from work on those days because you served on a jury during the week.
