FreshDirect will conduct business with absolute honesty and integrity. All employees must abide by certain work rules of general conduct and performance at all times. These work rules have been established in the best interest of the Company, its employees and its customers. They are intended to establish reasonable guidelines of what is expected from employees in their day-to-day conduct.

If you do not understand a rule or are unsure what to do in a given situation, ask your manager, supervisor, or a representative from the Human Resource Department. The rules listed below do not cover every situation and it is expected that you follow normal operating procedures and accepted standards of conduct in all situations. Any employee who violates any of the following rules shall subject be to disciplinary action up to and including discharge. Nothing in this policy modifies your status as an at-will employee.

Examples of misconduct may include but are not limited to:

  • Destroying, defacing or damaging Company property
  • Theft of time, including punching another employee’s time card or falsifying one’s own timecard (by claiming to have started work earlier or finished later than actually occurred)
  • Refusal to perform assigned work or follow a supervisor’s instructions
  • Dishonesty or falsifying your employment application or other Company records
  • Sleeping on the job or leaving one’s assigned work area without permission and while on the clock
  • Theft, attempted theft, or unauthorized possession of Company property or the property of client(s) or other employees, the receiving of such stolen property, or the unauthorized possession or removal of any such property from the premises without prior permission from senior management; this includes eating food on the job or removing/attempting to remove food from the building
  • Unauthorized use of Company equipment or property for personal reasons, or using Company equipment for profit
  • Possession or use of firearms, fireworks, explosives, knives (other than knives provided to butchers, chefs, etc.), or other dangerous weapons on Company premises or during working time
  • Failing to return from an approved leave of absence as scheduled, lying about the reasons for a leave, or working at another job in the same capacity while on an approved leave of absence
  • Violation of the Company’s equal opportunity or sexual harassment policies
  • Poor performance, inefficiency, or lack of effort on the job
  • Failure to service a client courteously and politely with a positive attitude
  • Walking off the job, ceasing work before the end of one’s shift without permission without authorization by your supervisor, or failure to start work on time
  • Performing work other than Company work during working hours
  • Removal from Company premises of Company property, equipment, goods or documents without prior approval of your supervisor
  • Willful deception with intent to cover up neglect of duty or violation of rules by the employee or any other employee
  • Not completing assignments up to the quality required by the Company
  • Failure to report promptly a work-related injury
  • Tape recording conversations/interactions (either audio/video) without approval of management
  • Use of cell phone or other technology to take pictures of (a) people in the workplace so as to invade their privacy or (b) any Company facilities for any reason
  • Gambling on Company premises
  • Post-hire conviction for the commission of a crime that would otherwise cause an employee to fail a pre-employment background check
  • Fighting, provoking a fight, horseplay, physical violence, disorderly conduct or lewd behavior, or engaging in intimidating behavior
  • Harassing another employee due to his/her race, color, religion, creed, sex, sexual orientation, age, ancestry, national origin, marital or domestic partnership status, military status, handicap, disability, or any other legally protected classification; this includes sexual harassment of another employee or making sexual advances or comments to fellow employees, visitors or clients
  • Receiving kickbacks or gifts for preferential treatment from anyone doing business with the Company
  • Smoking during times other than meal and break periods and smoking in non-designated areas
  • Excessive or pattern absenteeism or tardiness
  • Using threatening, profane, or abusive language to your manager, supervisor, fellow employee, or customer
  • Threatening, intimidating or coercing fellow employees on or off the premises at any time, for any purpose
  • Crediting your own account or colluding to credit another’s account for that person’s or your own benefit
  • Buying Company merchandise for resale
  • Speeding or careless driving of Company vehicles
  • Failure to immediately report damage to, or an accident involving, Company equipment
  • Disclosure of confidential or proprietary information about the Company or clients to the media and/or other unauthorized persons (this rule does not prohibit employees from speaking with others about their terms and conditions of employment but no employee may speak to the media on behalf of or about the Company without prior Company authorization)
  • Unethical conduct or serious conflicts of interest
  • Gaining unauthorized access to Company records
  • Violation of security or safety rules or failure to observe safety rules or FreshDirect safety practices; failure to wear required safety equipment; tampering with FreshDirect equipment or safety equipment
  • Negligence or any careless action which endangers the life or safety of another person
  • Being intoxicated or under the influence of a controlled substance while at work; use, possession or sale of a controlled substance in any quantity while on Company premises, except medications prescribed by a physician which do not impair work performance
  • Engaging in criminal conduct or acts of violence, or making threats of violence toward anyone on Company premises or when representing FreshDirect
  • Lying about sick or personal leave; falsifying the reason for a leave of absence or other data requested by FreshDirect; alteration of Company records or other Company documents
  • Creating or contributing to unsanitary conditions or violating Good Manufacturing Practices
  • Posting, removing, or altering notices on any bulletin board on Company property without the permission of the Human Resources Department of FreshDirect
  • Violation of any other Company policies
  • Knowingly assisting another employee or a third party in any misconduct

It is not possible to list every type of conduct that may result in disciplinary action. As such, the above list is not meant to be all-inclusive.
