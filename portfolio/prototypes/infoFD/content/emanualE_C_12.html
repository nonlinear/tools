All employees may be scheduled to work overtime when Company needs cannot be met during regular working hours. Whenever possible, advance notification will be provided. If determined necessary, overtime work will be authorized by management beyond an employee’s standard workweek. Non-exempt employees will be paid overtime compensation at the rate of time and one-half (1½) their straight-time rate for every hour worked over forty (40) per workweek. Overtime pay is based on actual hours worked. Paid time off (“PTO”) for vacation, personal days, holidays, or any other leave of absence will not be considered as hours worked when computing overtime. Any employee who feels that he of she has earned overtime pay but not been paid properly for it should contact Human Resources immediately.

Company management will endeavor to make overtime voluntary, and fill overtime needs with volunteers whenever possible. On rare occasions, this may not be possible, and all employees are expected to perform required overtime work as a condition of continued employment. Any employee who fails to work overtime as requested by management or works overtime without prior authorization from management may be subject to disciplinary action up to and including discharge. Only your immediate supervisor can authorize overtime work.
