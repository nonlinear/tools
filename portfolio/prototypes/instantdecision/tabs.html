We factor personal credit into our business lending decision because personal credit history is often longer than business credit history, and we want to make sure you don't have any overwhelming personal debt that can be a warning signal of trouble for business credit purposes.

670 Weighted Average Credit Score

On Deck calculates an average of the personal credit scores of the owners of the business, weighted by ownership percentage, to get a balanced sense of personal credit risk across business owners.
No Bankruptcy

A recent personal bankruptcy raises a warning flag, as it signals a failure to manage and keep debt obligations.
No Foreclosure

A foreclosure raises a warning flag, as it signals a failure to manage and keep debt obligations.
No Too Few Credit Accounts

Having too few credit accounts (credit cards, mortgages, auto financing, etc.) is a warning sign, as there is little historical credit payback experience to base future expectations upon.
No Less Than 48 Months of History

Having a short history is a warning sign, as there is little historical credit payback experience to base future expectations upon.
No 90+ Days Late, Ever

Historical payments that were late by 90 days or more are a warning sign for future payback issues.
No 60+ Days Late, Last 12 Months

Your business credit score and history gives us an indication of your past ability to pay back business credit. Like your personal credit, we look for public records, such as bankrupticies, liens, or judgments, that indicate that your business is not in a condition fit to be receiving more credit.

158 Business Credit Score

Your business credit score is a blended score calculated based on a number of different factors from your business and personal credit history.
No Bankruptcies

A business bankruptcy indicates to us that your business has experienced extreme stress and is not stable enough to take on debt.
Yes Liens

Outstanding liens on your business are a warning sign, as existing creditors to your business are seeking payback on their debt that will rank higher in priority to a new business loan.
No Judgements

Formal court decisions for plaintiffs seeking payback on their debt signal a failure to manage and keep debt obligations.
No Charge-offs

A charge-off is a declaration from another creditor that your debt has gone unpaid and they are declaring it as a loss, which is a warning sign for future business debt payback.
